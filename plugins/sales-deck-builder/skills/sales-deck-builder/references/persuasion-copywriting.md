# Persuasion and Copywriting Frameworks

Load when writing slide copy. Every headline, subhead, and body follows these principles.

## Core Framework: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

Applied across the ENTIRE deck arc:

1. **Problem** (slides 1-2): Name the pain. Be specific. Use the audience's language.
2. **Agitate** (slide 3): Make it worse. Show consequences — money, time, reputation lost. Quantify.
3. **Solve** (slides 4-9): Present your solution as obvious relief. Prove it works.

## Headline Formulas

### Value Headlines
- [Number] + [Outcome] + [Timeframe]: "3x Revenue in 6 Months"
- [Verb] + [Pain] + [Permanently]: "Eliminate Reporting Chaos for Good"
- [Outcome] + [Without] + [Sacrifice]: "Scale Without Hiring"

### Problem Headlines
- [You're] + [Losing] + [Amount]: "You're Burning $12K/Month on Manual QA"
- [Stinging question]: "When Was the Last Time You Shipped on Time?"
- [Shocking stat]: "68% of Sales Teams Miss Quota Every Quarter"

### Proof Headlines
- [Number] + [Who] + [Action]: "2,400 Teams Switched This Year"
- [Metric] + [Timeframe]: "93% Retention After 12 Months"

### CTA Headlines
- [Verb] + [Low Commitment]: "See It in Action — 15 Minutes"
- [Remove Objection] + [Action]: "No Card Required. Just Start."

## Body Copy Rules

### The 30-Word Rule
No slide body exceeds 30 words. If you can't say it in 30 words, you don't understand it.

### Sentence Structure
- Lead with benefit, not feature
- Active voice only: "We reduced churn by 40%" not "Churn was reduced"
- Present tense for capabilities: "Integrates with Slack" not "Can integrate"
- Kill filler: remove every "that", "very", "really", "just", "actually"

### Specificity Ladder
- Level 1 (weak): "We help companies grow"
- Level 2 (better): "We help SaaS companies grow revenue"
- Level 3 (strong): "We help SaaS companies grow MRR by 30-50%"
- Level 4 (best): "140 SaaS companies grew MRR by 30-50% in under 90 days"

Always aim for Level 3-4. If no real numbers, ask the user.

## Persuasion Triggers (one per slide, not all at once)

### Social Proof
- Client logos (min 4), testimonials with name/role/company, usage metrics, awards

### Scarcity and Urgency
- Limited time, limited spots, momentum ("47 onboarded this month")
- ONLY when true. Fake urgency destroys trust.

### Authority
- Team credentials, industry recognition, data sources, partnerships

### Risk Reversal
- Free trial, guarantee, no credit card, quick wins

## Transition Phrases (for speaker notes)

- Problem to Consequences: "And it gets worse..."
- Consequences to Solution: "But it doesn't have to be this way."
- Solution to How It Works: "Here's how simple it is."
- How It Works to Proof: "Don't take our word for it."
- Proof to Comparison: "So how does this stack up?"
- Comparison to Offer: "Here's what you get."
- Offer to CTA: "Ready to start?"

## Audience Adjustments

### Investors
- Lead with market insight, use financial language (ARR, MRR, CAC, LTV)
- Show trajectory, specific ask + use of funds

### Enterprise Buyers
- Risk reduction over innovation, ROI calculation mandatory
- Security/compliance visible, implementation timeline

### Internal Stakeholders
- Company metrics, acknowledge counterarguments, effort-to-impact ratio
- "Proposed next step" not hard CTA, include rollback plan

### Conference
- Open with surprise, teach something actionable
- No selling until last slide, close with "Monday morning action"

## Banned Patterns

- **Buzzword soup:** "innovative AI-powered cutting-edge platform"
- **Feature dumps without benefits**
- **"About Us" as slide 2** — earn attention first
- **Dead questions:** "Want to be more productive?" (vs "What would 12 free hours/week mean?")
- **"Thank You" ending** — end with CTA or memorable statement
- **Passive voice**
